A Letter to My Children

My dearest Lenny, Ray, and Marley,

If you are reading this, you are now old enough to understand the truth about what happened between your parents, and why your father was not present in your daily life for so many years.

I want you to know one thing above all else: I never stopped fighting for you. Not for a single day.

Every day, I sent your mother a message asking to speak with you. Every day, I called. Sometimes I got through for a few precious minutes. Most days, I was given an excuse — you were at a playdate, at a birthday party, in the shower, asleep, busy, or simply unavailable. When I did get through, the calls were cut short.

The court in Malta gave me the right to a daily video call with you. But rights on paper and reality were two very different things. In two months, I managed to speak with you only five times, for less than five minutes each.

I was not absent by choice. I was kept away.

Your grandparents — Savta Alla and Saba — they wanted to see you too. They love you more than words can express. They were also kept away.

This website exists so that one day, when you are ready, you can see for yourself: the messages I sent every single day, the court documents, the evidence. Everything here is documented and real.

I love you. I have always loved you. And I will never stop.

Your father,
Eitan
